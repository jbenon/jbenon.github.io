I am a PhD candidate in cognitive neuroscience at the Paris Brain Institute, supervised by Jean Daunizeau in the Motivation Brain Behavior team.

I am interested in the mechanisms of goal-directed behaviour. How to we decide where to allocate our attention and efforts, when do we switch? How do we choose between several courses of action?

To tackle these questions, I model computational and neuro-computational mechanisms involved in value-based decision-making.

My first paper focuses on the optimal effort allocation strategy for value synthesis. The other main project of my PhD involves using recurrent neural networks to simulate a value-synthesis system and look at its representational and neuronal properties under different constraints.
